## The Problem

You're on your laptop. You copy a link вҖ” a recipe, a GitHub repo, a meeting URL, whatever. Now you need it on your phone. So what do you do?

Email it to yourself? Open WhatsApp Web and send it to your own chat? Type it out letter by letter like it's 2005?

**ClipGuard fixes that.** Copy on Windows, it shows up on your iPhone within a second. Type something on your phone, it lands on your PC clipboard. Files too вҖ” drag and drop, done.

And here's the thing: **your data never leaves your house.** No cloud servers. No accounts. No analytics. Everything stays on your local Wi-Fi, between your own devices. That's it.

---

## вңЁ Features

**Bi-directional clipboard sync** вҖ” Copy anywhere on Windows (Ctrl+C in any app), and it appears on your iPhone instantly. Send text from your phone, and it's on your PC clipboard ready to paste.

**Dual-panel interface** вҖ” Separate boxes for sending (iPhone вҶ’ Windows) and receiving (Windows вҶ’ iPhone). Each with its own Clear button. No confusion about what went where.

**File transfer** вҖ” Pick files on your iPhone and upload them to your PC. Download files from your PC to your phone. Progress bars and everything.

**One-tap authentication** вҖ” Scan a QR code with your iPhone camera, type a 6-digit PIN, done. New PIN every 5 minutes. No passwords to remember.

**Works offline** вҖ” Install as a PWA on your iPhone (Add to Home Screen in Safari). Once installed, it works without opening Safari.

**Clipboard history** вҖ” Every synced item is logged with timestamps. Scroll back and copy anything again.

**Auto-reconnect** вҖ” Wi-Fi hiccup? Phone went to sleep? ClipGuard reconnects automatically with exponential backoff. Falls back to HTTP polling if WebSocket drops.

The server runs a background thread that checks your Windows clipboard every 600 milliseconds. When it detects a change вҖ” you copied something new вҖ” it broadcasts the text to every connected iPhone over WebSocket.

Going the other direction, your iPhone sends text through the WebSocket (or falls back to HTTP POST if the socket drops), and the server writes it to your Windows clipboard using `pyperclip`.

**Echo prevention** is the tricky part. When your iPhone sends text to Windows, `pyperclip.copy()` writes it to the clipboard вҖ” which the monitor thread then detects as a "new" clipboard change and tries to broadcast it back. Infinite loop. ClipGuard breaks this cycle with three layers: MD5 hash comparison, a directional origin flag, and a 2-second timestamp debounce window.

## рҹҗӣ Troubleshooting

**iPhone shows OFFLINE**<br>
Tap the status badge to force a reconnect. Make sure both devices are on the same Wi-Fi network. If it keeps happening, go to iPhone Settings вҶ’ Safari вҶ’ Clear Website Data for your server IP.

**Windows вҶ’ iPhone isn't showing**<br>
Check the terminal вҖ” you should see `в–І WinвҶ’iOS (1 clients)` when you copy something. If you see `(0 clients)`, the iPhone isn't connected. Reconnect. If you see the broadcast but the phone doesn't update, go to Settings tab on the phone and check the message counter.

**iPhone вҶ’ Windows works but the other way doesn't**<br>
Usually a stale service worker cache. On iPhone: Settings вҶ’ Safari вҶ’ Advanced вҶ’ Website Data вҶ’ find your server IP вҶ’ Delete. Then reconnect.

**`ConnectionResetError` in the terminal**<br>
Normal. This just means a previous WebSocket connection was closed (phone went to sleep, tab switched, etc.). The phone auto-reconnects.

**PIN keeps expiring**<br>
PINs last 5 minutes. Click "New PIN" on the dashboard. Once authenticated, the session stays active вҖ” you don't need a new PIN until you disconnect.

## вқ“ FAQ

**Can I deploy this on Vercel / Netlify / the cloud?**

No. ClipGuard reads your Windows clipboard using `pyperclip`, which needs a Python process running on your actual computer. Cloud platforms can't access your desktop. This is a local tool by design вҖ” and that's a feature, not a limitation. Your clipboard data stays in your house.

**Is this secure?**

Your data never leaves your local network. The PIN-based auth prevents random devices on your Wi-Fi from connecting. For a home network, this is plenty secure. If you're on a shared/public Wi-Fi, don't use this (but you probably shouldn't be syncing clipboards on public Wi-Fi anyway).

**Does it work on Mac?**

Should work вҖ” `pyperclip` supports macOS. Haven't tested it extensively, but the architecture is platform-agnostic. Give it a shot and open an issue if something breaks.

**Can I connect multiple phones?**

Yes. The server broadcasts to all connected clients simultaneously. Each device sees the same clipboard updates in real time.

**What about Android?**

It should work in Chrome on Android вҖ” the web app is just HTML/JS. The clipboard API behavior might differ slightly. Haven't tested it yet.
